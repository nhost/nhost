We're actively working on implementing user feedback by improving the billing experience on our platform. These improvements aim to make billing more predictable, transparent, and user-friendly. Additionally, they lay the groundwork for exciting new features, such as preview environments, the ability to transfer projects between organizations, and the option to pause paid projects when needed. 

As part of these updates, we are renaming workspaces to organizations and shifting from a project-based billing model to an organization-based billing one. This change will provide a more streamlined and scalable approach, especially for users managing multiple projects within the same organization.

## Deprecating workspaces in favor of organizations

When we launched V2, we introduced Workspaces as a way to organize projects. However, after listening to user feedback and seeing industry trends, we are moving back to Organizations to better align with users' expectations and industry standards.

Organizations function similarly to workspaces, allowing you to group projects together. Organizations can also accommodate multiple members with different roles.

Your existing workspaces will remain accessible on the Nhost Dashboard until February 1st, 2025. During this time you won't be able to edit or create new ones. We encourage you to transition to organizations as soon as possible to take full advantage of the new mprovements.

## Moving from project-based to organization-based billing

![Project vs Organization billing](/images/blog/og-project-vs-org-billing.png)

Currently, each project has its own plan and subscription, which has led to frustration for many of you due to the number of invoices involved and the complexity of managing billing across multiple projects.

To simplify the billing process and address these concerns, we are transitioning from project-based billing to organization-based billing. Going forward, each organization will have a single plan — Starter, Pro, Team, or Enterprise — that covers all associated projects. This includes any additional resources or features tied to those projects, such as software add-ons like [Graphite](https://docs.nhost.io/product/ai), or platform features like [Compute Resources](https://docs.nhost.io/compute-resources) and [Custom Domains](https://docs.nhost.io/platform/custom-domains).

This means fewer invoices and a simpler billing experience: one plan, one invoice, covering everything under your organization.

### Quotas & Featured shared across organizations

No matter how many projects you manage within an organization, the included quota remains the same across all of them. For billing purposes, we aggregate the usage from all projects under the organization and reflect it in a single, consolidated invoice.

Upgrading an organization to a paid plan unlocks the benefits of that plan for every project within the organization. For example, if you upgrade your organization to the Team plan, you’ll gain access to advanced GraphQL metrics and other premium features for all projects associated with that organizationu — without needing to upgrade each project individually.

### Billing & Billing Cycles

Under the current project-based billing, when you create a project on a paid plan, billing starts immediately. However, with the new organization-based billing system, you will only be charged after creating an organization on a paid plan, and billing for all resources associated with projects in that organization will occur at the end of each month.

To help you get started, we offer $15 worth of credits for every paid organization you create. These credits are sufficient to cover one project with shared compute for a full month, or they can be applied toward dedicated compute if purchased.


A few things to note:

- Billing for projects applies only to compute resources (shared or dedicated), add-ons, and any quotas that are exceeded.
- You can create and delete projects as needed, and you'll only be charged for the time your projects are actively running — compute is billed by the minute.
- For example, if you have two projects within a Pro organization, you will only pay $40 instead of $50 as of today — meaning we’re making it cheaper in most scenarios.
- You will be charged based on actual usage at the end of the billing cycle, rather than paying the full monthly cost upfront.

### Migration

All Starter projects will be automatically migrated by us to the new organization-based billing. However, for paid projects, you will need to handle the migration yourself. Don’t worry — this process will be very easy, requiring just a couple of clicks. Additionally, you’ll have plenty of time to complete the migration.

We are aiming to have everything ready and released on October 27th. If we decide to delay the launch we will let you know.
