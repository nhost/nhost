## Introduction

React Flow is a small company based in Berlin that focuses on developing a frontend library for building node-based interfaces, workflow editors, and interactive diagrams. The library is widely used, and to secure its financial stability, the company has introduced a subscription payment model powered by Nhost.

## Problem

The team at React Flow consists of frontend developers with limited backend knowledge. Therefore, they needed a solution to handle complicated auth functionality with ease.

## Solution

The team chose Nhost over its competitors as it integrated well with React Flow's stack and offered a variety of features through its SDK and API, including GraphQL support. The startup wanted to support other early-stage startups, and Nhost, being based in the EU, was an added bonus. The active support team and founders on Discord with fast response times were crucial in ensuring React Flow's confidence in its choice.

## Benefits of Nhost

Using Nhost enabled React Flow to build its subscription platform in just two months with one frontend developer working on it. The team made heavy use of the NextJS integration and React SDK, and access to the Hasura console was helpful for debugging. The ability to test features and make changes in the dev environment before deploying remotely was crucial in ensuring the quality of the development.

## Conclusion

React Flow would recommend Nhost to other developers or teams, especially frontend (React) developers who need reliable authentication and database for small to medium projects. The pricing and billing are reasonable, especially when compared to the cost of building and maintaining the platform on its own. Nhost is an affordable and well-documented solution with outstanding support and team.
